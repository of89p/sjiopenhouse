This open house website is a student initiated project

Long-exposure photography: Freezing the still subject in the picture while blurring the objects in constant motion. Tail lights of cars become a strip of red and the moon can be in multiple positions in the sky.

Life moves all too fast. Look: Even our faces blur in the unison with motion of the world.

"Oh Shit! I missed 156!"

Oh shit, indeed.

What is the point of all the chaos, the endless motion?

But it's not just motion outside, but inside too.

Perhaps the shake comes from the presence of another?

I don't know, you tell me.

But in all seriousness, motion is a strange thing isn't it? From point A you go to B.

But time is a cunning motion, for once past a point you cannot go back to it. Isn't this the fuel for reminiscence and nostalgia? They are nothing but inherent annoyances caused by the motion of time.

"I can only note that the past is beautiful because one never realises an emotion at the time. It expands later, and thus we don't have complete emotions about the present, only about the past."

One of my favourite quotes, by Virginia Woolf.

Which thus raises the question: How do you know if you truly are happy? After all, the purest and most honest emotions comes later on anyways, never living in the moment itself.

I don't know, neither do you I think. So go with the flow. Should time be a constant motion, turning and turning like a carousel, then hop on in the ride.

But bring your camera. Photograph your friend in the seat beside you. He is moving at the same speed as you. So in the picture, your friend would remain in focus, while the background blurs into thin strips of colourful lights.

In this world of motion, capturing the stills is all that matters. What better way to do so than taking pictures?
